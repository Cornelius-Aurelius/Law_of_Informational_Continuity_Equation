Simple Explanation

Information cannot vanish or appear out of nowhere — it can only move. The Informational Continuity Law describes how information density flows through a system, ensuring conservation.

If information decreases somewhere, it must have flowed to another place. Just like mass, charge, or probability, *information is conserved*.

Scientific Explanation

The informational density \( \rho(x,t) \) and informational flux \( J(x,t) \) satisfy the continuity equation:

\( \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} + \nabla \cdot J = 0 \)

This law guarantees that informational mass is preserved under evolution, and that all changes in density arise from flow, not creation or destruction.

This is a foundational requirement for informational physics, PDE systems, wave laws, diffusion laws, and the δJ equilibrium model.

Formal Expression

\( \frac{\partial \rho}{\partial t} = - \nabla \cdot J \)

\( \int \rho(x,t)\,dx = \text{constant} \)
